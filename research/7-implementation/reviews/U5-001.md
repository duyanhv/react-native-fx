## Contract reconciliation

- **Internal read surface** (`FxLayoutObserver.swift:43-78` / `FxLayoutObserver.kt:55-89`):
  parent-space frame (captured), window origin, edge travel, safe-area insets — the four
  reads the spec names; synchronous, native, read-on-demand. Window-space reads are live
  (ancestor scroll moves the view without a local layout event) — pinned rationale.
- **Ownership + lifecycle**: created with `FxSurfaceView` (`FxSurfaceView.swift:80-90`,
  `FxSurfaceView.kt:85`), iOS `invalidate()` in `deinit`; Android lifetime view-bound
  (the view holds the listener on itself) with `detach()` as the explicit teardown surface.
- **Rules gate**: #9 — reads only, no `setNeedsLayout`/`requestLayout` anywhere in either
  file; #1 — captures are event-driven (KVO / layout-change listener), no polling, no JS;
  #7 — pure Swift/Kotlin, the ObjC++ `updateLayoutMetrics` path explicitly rejected and the
  rejection pinned; #3 — `expo-view` only. Scope boundaries held: no U6 animation, no U7
  FSM, no public prop/event, U4 child-routing and Metal paths untouched beyond the two
  ownership lines.
- **Structure pins precede code** and carry the exact callbacks, coordinate spaces, and
  template file:line (`UIView+ComponentViewProtocol.mm:84-110`,
  `SwiftUIViewFrameObserver.swift:16-31`, `SurfaceMountingManager.kt:829-859`,
  `RNHostView.kt:131-135`, `ReactSurfaceView.kt:50-70`).
