## Review rounds (implementation, 2026-06-11)

Round 1 found four defects; all were fixed and re-verified in round 2:

1. **Stale render-server provenance on a second retarget** — `renderServerStartTime`
   survived the first handoff, so a second retarget mid-display-link would re-predict
   velocity from the dead render-server envelope. Fixed: provenance cleared at handoff
   (`FxAnimationDriver.swift:130-132`); the device scenario gained the second-retarget
   regression check on the planner's instruction.
2. **Fire-once spring duration mismatched the `FxSpring` solver** — the render-server
   envelope and the integrator disagreed on settling time. Fixed: the fire-once path
   commits `FxSpring().duration` (`FxAnimationDriver.swift:102-104`).
3. **Event-dispatch scope violation** — completion initially routed toward
   `onFxTransitionEnd`; presence semantics are U7's. Fixed: completion is the internal
   `onContentAnimationCompletion` hook (`FxSurfaceView.swift:83`, `FxSurfaceView.kt:88`),
   the exact attachment point the U7-001 spec now consumes.
4. **Missing iceberg comments** on the non-obvious mechanics (handoff provenance, velocity
   prediction, model-layer writes for hit-testing). Fixed; Android pause also made
   non-destructive in the same round.

## Device evidence reconciliation (the five points + the regression check)

Against `evidence/headless.md`, on POCO F1 / Android 15 API 35 and iPhone 17 Pro
simulator / iOS 26.5:

- **Fire-once, completion exactly once** — one completion log per envelope on both
  platforms, settled at the exact target vector.
- **Mid-flight retarget, no snap, velocity carried** — iOS logged the render-server →
  display-link handoff with the captured presentation value
  (`presentation=…translationX=56.833` en route to 160); Android logged
  `animateToFinalPosition()` per property. The recordings show no snap or jump.
- **The second-retarget regression check (round 1's bug)** — the second retarget logs
  `source=display-link` with live carried velocity (`translationX=-816.602`), targets only
  the final vector, and exactly one completion lands at it.
- **The opposing-inertia clip, observed on device** — at the first handoff the predicted
  translationX velocity pointed at the old target (+160) while the new target lay at
  -120, so the clip zeroed it (`carriedVelocity translationX=0.000`) while same-direction
  opacity carried (`-2.557`). This is `FxSpring.clippingOpposingInertia` behaving exactly
  as ratified — the log line is direct device proof of the mechanism.
- **Wrapped RN child tappable at rest** — the child's normal RN handler fired
  (`child tap count=1`, both platforms).
- **Cancel settles in place** — Android `activeBefore=4 activeAfter=0`; iOS
  `displayLinkWasActive=false renderServerWasActive=true`, settled mid-flight values, no
  completion after cancel.
- **Reduce-motion / animator-scale-0** — single-frame placement, one completion, settings
  restored after the run.

Anomalies disclosed in the evidence (a pre-marker Android soft exception; one missed iOS
tap against a debugger affordance, re-run as a corrected block) do not implicate the
driver.
