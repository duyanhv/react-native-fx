## Verdict

**Approved.** Native-owned drag/tilt ships on both platforms — a native recognizer reads the
gesture and writes the uniform natively every frame (no per-frame JS, no Reanimated, rule #1/#7).
The closing hardware gate PASSED by finger on real devices (iPhone 14 / iOS 26.5 + POCO F1 /
Android 15, 2026-06-18). **RT-002 closes here**: `30` G3 flipped open→shipped, the mechanic pinned
in `structure.{ios,android}.md` § Touch contract, ledger RT-002 `deferred`→`resolved`.

The three design forks were settled (planner + maintainer, 2026-06-17): `active` + `dragAxis?:
'horizontal'|'vertical'|'both'` (no new `interactionMode` value, honoring the DEF-015 freeze);
`drag`/`tilt` as vec2 signed `[-1,1]` in the shipped touch-UV space (`drag = touchUV − originUV`,
axis-masked; `tilt = (touchUV − 0.5)*2`, full-2D); the iOS dual-path ABI cost accepted.

## Arbitration spike (sim/emulator, 2026-06-17 — `evidence/device.md`)

Verdict: **not falsified — proceed to Phase 2**, but Android-decisive, NOT both-platforms (the
executor's "all pass" headline overstated it; corrected by the planner against the screenshots).
Proven: Android S1 (claim horizontal + yield vertical) and S3 (`both` blocks the parent scroller) —
the two decisive corners. Not proven on the sim: Android S2/S4/S5 (offscreen / inner-scroll
position unreadable via a11y) and **all iOS** (XCTest cannot observe recognizer state; Phase 1 has
no visible uniform, so claiming is invisible). These were tooling/visibility limits, not failures —
closed by Phase 2's visible uniform + the hardware gate. Spike finding (rule #6): iOS claims by
keeping its recognizer alive while the scroller always scrolls (simultaneous recognition); Android
claims by blocking the parent (`requestDisallowInterceptTouchEvent`). `horizontal`/`vertical`
converge; `both`-inside-a-scroller diverges — settled in Phase 2 as `both` standalone-only on iOS.

## Closing hardware gate — PASS (human, real devices, 2026-06-18 — `evidence/device-hardware.md`)

Fresh native builds at HEAD + the gate fix below. All scenarios verified by finger: S1/S2/S4/S5 axis
claiming, drag/tilt tracking the finger with no per-frame JS under load, native settle, W-F2, W-F3
(no scroll-start hitch from the Android `ACTION_DOWN` disallow), the `@gorhom`/RNGH coexistence
cases, loop pauses off-window. The 0.35-easing watch (uniform lagging the finger a few frames during
active drag) read as smooth, not laggy.

- **Ratified platform divergence:** the iOS `both` case keeps the ancestor scroll active — `both` is
  **standalone-only on iOS** (out of scope, not a bug; rule #4). Android blocks the parent for `both`.
- **Gate bug found + fixed (`0b4f9de`):** iOS dots vanished mid-drag because
  `UILongPressGestureRecognizer`'s built-in `allowableMovement` (10 pt default) auto-failed the
  recognizer before the axis-aware `shouldFail` could arbitrate — set to `.greatestFiniteMagnitude`
  so `shouldFail` is the sole arbiter. The S3 demo was relabeled standalone-only (`49e89f6`).
